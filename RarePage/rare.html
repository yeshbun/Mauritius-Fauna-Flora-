Mauritius Fauna & Flora Extinct Endemic Vulnerable Rare


Rare species are groups of organisms that are very uncommon, scarce, or infrequently encountered. This designation may be applied to either a plant or animal taxon, and is distinct from the term endangered or threatened.

Rare Species

Green Turtle

Common name: Turtle Scientific name: Chelonia mydas

Did you know? The endangered Green Turtle is particularly at risk. There are several turtle breeding habitats on the large, isolated sandy beaches. The turtle, whose nursing grounds are in the Blue Bay Marine Park and along the East Coast of Mauritius use the pristine, sandy beaches to bury their eggs during nesting season. The long term consequences on vulnerable turtle populations – particularly the eggs and hatchlings - by the fine, toxic particles of heavy oil interspersed with the sand, is yet to be fully understood.

Can be seen at: Northern Lagoon

Lotus Flower

Common name: Lotus Scientific name: Nelumbo nucifera Size: 26cm

Did you know? The lotus flower is an aquatic perennial. Sometimes mistaken for the water-lily, the lotus has a distinctively different structure. It also only comes in pink hues or white, whereas the lily comes in many different colors. The roots are implanted in the soil of a river or pond, and the leaves float on the surface. Nelumbo nucifera, also known as Indian lotus, sacred lotus, or simply lotus, is one of two extant species of aquatic plant in the family Nelumbonaceae. It is sometimes colloquially called a water lily, though this more often refers to members of the family Nymphaeaceae.

Can be seen at: SSR Botcanical Gardens

Pink Mandrinette Hibiscus

Common name: Mandrinette Scientific name:Hibiscus fragilis Height: 50 cm

Did you know? Pink Mandrinette Hibiscus is an extremly rare and highly endangered, evergreen wild species from Mauritius. It has a slightly prostrate habit and bears small leaves and ornamental, and for size of the plant rather large, rich pink flowers..

Flowering and fruiting: The plants germinate unevenly over a period of several months.

Can be seen at: Corps de Garde and Le Morne Brabant

Jamrosal

Common name: Jean Bourgeois

Scientific name:Syzygium jambos Tree Height: 15 m

Did you know? Syzygium jambos is a species of rose apple originating in Southeast Asia and occurring widely elsewhere, having been introduced as an ornamental and fruit tree. The flowers are described by some as fragrant, though this appears to be a variable attribute. The ripe fruit, however, has a strong, pleasant floral bouquet, hence such common names as "Rose apple" and "pomarrosa

Flowering and fruiting: The tree will flower midsummer, with fruits ripening from 3-4 months later.

Can be seen at: Les Mariannes
